What is Mental Health?

According to the cdc: "Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make healthy choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood."

What are some Mental Health issues?

Anxiety Disorders - respond to situations with fear and anxiety in a way that is not normal

Mood Disorders - emotional state is inconsistent and they can often feel either extremely happy or extremely sad

Psychotic Disorders - distorted awarness and/or thinking; this may include having hallucinations or delusions, etc.

Eating Disorders - extreme behaviors involving weight and food; ex. Anorexianervousa, bulimia nervosa, etc.

Impulse/Addiction Disorders - unable to resists urges and performs acts that can harm themselves; ex. pyromania, kleptomania, compulsive gambling, etc.

Personality Disorders - have extreme and inflexible personality traits that interferes with work, school and relationships; their thinking and behavior is extremely different from expectations

Obsessive-compulsize Disorders(OCD) - constant thoughts/or fears that causes them to perform a certain action or routine; ex. someone who constatly washes their hands because of their fear of germs

Post-traumatic stress Disorders(PTSD) - condition that develops following a traumatic experience; leaves a lasting and frightening thought of the memory, etc.
