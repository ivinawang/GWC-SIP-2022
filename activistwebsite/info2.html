Mental Health Myths vs. Facts

Myth: Mental health problems don't affect me.

Mental health problems are actually very common. In fact, one in five American adults experienced a mental health issue.


Myth: Children don't experience mental health problems.

From a very young age, those with metnal illnessess can show early warning of mental illnesses; and can be clinically diagnosable. Half of most mental health disorders show first signs before 14 years old- however, only half of those children recieve proper treatment.


Myth: People with mental health problems are violent and unpredictable.

Most people with mental health problesm are no more likely to be violent than anyone else; only 3-5% pf violents ats are attributed to those with a mental health issue


Myth: Therapy and self-help are a waste of time. Why bother when you can just take a pill?

Treatment differs depending on the illness: it could include medication, therapy, or both.


Myth: I can't do anything for a person with a mental health problem.

Friend and family can make a big difference; Whether it is encouraing them to take treatment or sharing factsa about mental health, etc.
